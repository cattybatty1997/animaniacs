Anime/Manga Lingo

aniki
big brother (not usually an actual brother, just a close friend)
baka
idiot, stupid
bento
boxed lunch
bishoujo
beautiful girl
bishounen
beautiful boy
chan
used when talking to someone familiar, usually a more "cute" or feminine way of saying one's name
chibi
child, usually a style of drawing characters with large heads and small bodies
cosplay
anime fans dressing up as characters, often with costumes they make themselves
daisuki
Japanese for "I love you"
desu
means "to be", used to add a unique characteristic to a character's speech or personality
doujinshi
short one-shot mangas drawn by amateurs and fans based on popular manga and anime
ecchi
genre with lots of fanservice, sexual hints/thoughts and skin showing often in a comical manner or creating a funny situation
eroge
games that feature erotic content in form of anime-style artwork
hotpot
raw meat cooked in a stew
fan service
refers to sexual hints, basically sexual eye-candy that is shown in almost every Anime/manga series to a certain extent
fansubs
anime translated by fan-via subtitles, released on the internet for free
hai
japanese for "yes"
haru
japanese for "Spring"
harem
genre that includes love comedy shows that feature three or more girls who fall in love with the male protagonist
hentai
means "pervert", genre of pornographic anime or manga
ichigo
japanese meaning "strawberries"
iie
japanese for "no"
iku
japanese for "let's go!" or "go away!" depending on conjugation
itadakimasu
always said before eating, often translated to "let's eat"
ittekimasu
often said when leaving the house for work or for school "I'll go and come back"
itterashai
often said when someone else is leaving the house for work or for school meaning "Please go and come back"
josei
genre that targets women and older teens, features realistic romance stories about adulthood, growing up, and college, has female protagonists
J-Pop
short for Japanese Pop or Japanese popular music
kanji
Chinese characters that are used in the modern Japanese logographic writing system
karaoke
Japanese pastime of singing pop songs with the original music without the lyrics playing in the background
katakana
one of the four Japanese writing systems, characterized by short straight strokes and angular corners, simplest of Japanese scripts
kawaii
Japanese for "cute"
kendo
Japanese martial art using a wooden sword
komoi
Japanese for "disgusting"
kimono
traditional Japanese clothing
konbawa
Japanese for "good evening"
konnichiwa
Japanese for "hello"
kouhai
underclassmen or younger co-worker
lolicon
anime pictures and manga that feature young girls
mahou shoujo
genre with witches or sorceresses with magical powers
manga
Japanese comic books
mangaka
professional manga authors
mecha
genre that involves mechanical things, mostly about fighting giant robots piloted by a single human
minna
Japanese word meaning "everyone/everybody"
moe
Japanese slang that describes something young, naive, cute, and innocent
nani
Japanese for "what"
neko
Japanese for "cat"
ojisan
term of endearment used to address a grandfather or uncle, but could also be used insult or make fun of middle-aged men for being old
okaasan
used to address you mother
okinawa
group of islands in the south of Japan, most popular is the Okinawa Island, which is a popular vacation spot
omake
short bonus chapter of a manga series or special features on an anime DVD
onee-san
older sister
oni-san
older brother
otaku
obsessed Japanese anime and manga fans
OVA
original video animation, Japanese anime exclusively sold as DVD, and do not air in Japanese television
otousan
used to address a father
romanji
romanization of Japanese words
sama
lord, master, someone of higher standing
sakura
Japanese term for pink cherry blossoms, symbolizes spring
sayonara
Japanese for "good-bye"
scanlation
term used for original Japanese manga that is scanned and translated by fans, typically distributed on the internet for free
seinen
genre that is targeted at men and older male teens, deals with darker, deeper, and more realistic themes and characters as in adulthood and the working world
shinigami
means "Death God" or any god associated with death
shoujo
genre targeted at a young female audience and usually features a romance story with a female protagonist
shoujo-ai
genre that deals with homosexual relationships between females
smut
genre that features some sexual scenes
suki
Japanese term that means "to love"
sumimasen
Japanese for "Excuse me" or "I'm sorry"
tadaima
Japanese for "I'm home"
tomodachi
Japanese for "friend"
tsundere
someone who is usually cold and calculating at first, but becomes a nicer, more vulnerable person as you get to know them
usotiki
Japanese for "liar"
watashi
Japanese for "I"
White Day
celebrated on March 14th where the boys give the girls gifts in return, like a Valentine's Day
yakusoku
Japanese for "promise" or "oath"
yandere
characters that seem calm in the beginning, but eventually become psychotic and violent
yaoi
genre that deals with homosexual relationships between men
yuri
genre that deals with female homosexual relationships, more graphic that shoujo-ai
