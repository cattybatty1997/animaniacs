How to become an Animaniac

Anime are Japanese animated productions featuring hand-drawn or computer animation. It is primarily used for animated televesion series, feature films and Internet-based releases. For more information on anime, visit this site.

Manga are comics created in Japan, or by Japanese creators (called mangaka) in the Japanese langauage. In Japan manga appeals to people of all ages, and like anime, includes works in a broad range of genres. For more information on manga, visit this site.

Step 1: Pick Your Favorite Genres

The Anime/Manga world offers a broad spectrum of genres to choose from. Look at some of the examples below and pick your favorites or explore some new ones:

Sci-Fi/Fantasy
This is just what you expect it to be. Mahou shoujo and mecha are possible sub-genres that you could look at.
Action and Adventure
Anime that is under the action and adventure, are more concentrated on fighting and exploring. This genre usually has less fan service,and more fighting than other genres.
Mystery
Your typical mystery movies and cartoons, usually involving some supernatural aspects.
Romance/Romantic Comedies
There are several different sub-genres within this category including: ecchi, harem, shoujo, shoujo-ai, yaoi, and yuri.
Horror/Thriller
There aren't many horror anime, and most are in the form of a movie than a series.
Comedy
A commonly used genre, it is usually paired with a romance manga as a romantic comedy.
Step 2: How to Read (Manga)

Reading manga is not the same as reading any other book or graphic novel. First off, you have to read from the right cover to the left cover. Also, you have to read from top to bottom, right to left. It's important to remember that right to left always beats top to bottom. Here are some sites where you can read manga:

  • Manga4
  • Manga Reader
  • Animea
Step 3: Anime

It's easy to find anime to watch; if you liked a manga, there may be an anime with the same name. Here are some sites where you can watch anime:

  • Crunchyroll (This one is the safest, but it does have a lot of advertisements)
  • AFTV (This one has more options, but it may not necessarily be the safest)
  • Watch Cartoon Online
Step 4: Fandoms

Now that you have (hopefully) found a favorite anime or manga, what next? Well, the adventure still continues of course! Being part of a fandom means purchasing merchandise, browsing through tumblr or other known sites that support fandoms, reading or writing fanfiction, and of course COSPLAYING!

Congratulations! You are now an ANIMANIAC!
